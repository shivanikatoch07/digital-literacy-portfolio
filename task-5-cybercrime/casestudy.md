## Cybercrime Case Study: Phishing

Phishing is one of the most common types of cybercrime where attackers trick users into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs. It usually occurs through fake emails, messages, or websites that appear to be from trusted sources like banks or companies.

In a typical phishing attack, a user receives a message creating urgency, such as “Your account will be blocked” or “Verify your details immediately.” The message includes a link that redirects to a fake website. When the user enters their information, it is stolen by the attacker.

Students and beginners are often targeted because they may not be fully aware of such scams. This can lead to serious consequences like financial loss, identity theft, and misuse of personal data.

This case highlights the importance of being cautious while using digital platforms. Users should always verify links and avoid sharing sensitive information online.
